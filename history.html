History of Neemuch

Neemuch (or Nimach) is a historic town located in the Malwa region of Madhya Pradesh. It has played an important role in India’s military and economic history.

British Cantonment & CRPF Origins

Neemuch was a British cantonment and later became the birthplace of the Central Reserve Police Force (CRPF) in 1939. The city still hosts one of the largest CRPF training centers in India.

Opium & Agriculture Trade Hub

Neemuch has historically been one of India’s major opium markets and an agricultural trade hub. Its strategic location connected major trade routes between Rajasthan and central India.

Modern-Day Neemuch

Neemuch was carved out from Mandsaur in 1998 to become its own district. It has since grown with modern infrastructure, schools, hospitals, and green initiatives that reflect its name — “A City of Nature.”
